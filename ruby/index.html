The problem in the peer economy

Laura, a freelancer, agreed to design a logo for Jim for 200€. Upon delivery, Jim considered the work was not up to standard. Therefore, refused to pay. Both want to dispute it, but litigation and arbitration are too expensive for the money in play. There is no efficient legal solution to their argument.

The parties to a dispute

With Parius the parties can explain their view on the facts of the case and enter Proposals with what they are expecting to be awarded in the dispute resolution. Two rounds of proposals allow the parties to provide compromise-proposals as nothing is merely black or white.

The voters

The same community without a legal solution will work to create their own justice. A random selection of jurors will have an economic incentive to vote for the proposal they expect the majority would vote, impartially aligning them with the truth. Voters will work to accumulate reputation in order to have a bigger weight in the decision.

Enforcement

Parius’ dispute resolution mechanism will be designed for perfect compatibility with P2P marketplaces, as well as, smart contracts. Thus allowing for fast, efficient and automatic enforcement for the winning party without the need of new enforcement proceedings.
